About NameRank

Definition

For entity e, disambiguating context c(e), gold answer g(e), and a model panel M of size 37,

NameRank(e) = (1/|M|) · Σm∈M cov(re,m, g(e)) · acc(re,m, g(e))

where re,m is the response of model m to the open-ended probe and cov, acc ∈ [0,1] are independent coverage and accuracy scores produced by an LLM judge against the curated gold answer. The multiplication is critical: a fluent-hallucination response with high topical coverage but factually wrong specifics has cov → 1 but acc → 0, yielding NameRank contribution → 0 for that (entity, model) pair.

Probe template

Tell me what you know about {name}, who is {context}.
If you do not recognize this entity, respond with "unknown".
Limit your response to about 150 words.

Judge

Gemini 3 Flash Preview (direct Google API, not via OpenRouter, to minimize routing variance) at temperature 0 and low reasoning budget. The judge prompt elicits two independent [0,1] scores: coverage (how much of the gold's substantive content appears in the response) and accuracy (are the factual claims correct). Refusals score 0 on both axes. The Gemini family-bias gap, measured on a stratified n=200 sample, is +0.006 ± 0.034 — statistically zero.

External validity

On the OpenAlex long-tail-researcher cohort (n=771, citations 500–30,000): log(h-index) explains R²=0.40 of NameRank variance; log(citations) explains R²=0.14; their joint R² stays at 0.40. Citation volume adds zero marginal predictive power beyond h-index. Out-of-sample (70/30 split): R²=0.498, Pearson 0.719. The mechanistic interpretation: h-index measures the distribution of named-paper output, which is what a name-attribution metric should track; raw citations are dominated by mega-author papers with weak per-author attribution.

Variance decomposition

Across 211,603 records: entity-attributable variance is 35.8 %; model bias is 10.9 %; cohort is 16.8 %. σ²entity/σ²model = 3.3 — NameRank measures the entity, not the panel. The empirical panel-size sensitivity curve (Pearson correlation against the full 37-model panel as a function of subset size k) and the per-model generosity/refusal breakdown are on the validation page.

Threshold cleanliness

A contemporaneously-published GPT-5 system-card author cohort (n=80) registers mean NameRank 0.042 with 71% silent responses — confirming the metric is silent rather than misleading below the elite recognition threshold.

What NameRank does not measure

Quality, merit, or intrinsic impact. The clearest case: tuixue.online has ~1M Chinese-language users — measurable, indisputable human reach — but registers NameRank 0.25 because the URL does not co-travel with its creator's name in indexable text. NameRank correctly registers low here, not because the artifact lacks impact, but because the metric measures something specific: the propagation of a specific name into the indexed corpora that frontier-model training pipelines scrape.
