Cross-language deltas

240 entities were re-probed with a Chinese-translated probe template (gold answer left in English; judge handles cross-language). The pattern is consistent: Chinese-character names lift on Chinese prompts; English-coded artifacts (FlashAttention, ReAct) lose. The lift is dominated by entity-name match, not model nationality.

Show:
EntityCohort ENZH Δ (zh − en) EN/WEN/C ZH/WZH/C

W = mean across 23 Western-aligned panel models; C = mean across 14 Chinese-aligned panel models. Both classes lift slightly on Chinese prompts (Western average +0.014; Chinese average +0.028); the dominant signal is entity-name match.
