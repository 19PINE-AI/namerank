NameRank

A continuous [0,1] recognition score for people and named artifacts in the LLM era. NameRank operationalizes the 65% recognition-variance residual that bibliometrics cannot explain (Li 2026, IKP §5.7) into a single number, computed against a 37-model frontier panel. 5,719 entities, 54 cohorts, 211,603 probe records, plus an 8,880-record Chinese-prompt sub-run.

Five headline findings

  1. The credential treadmill. IMO gold (0.36), Rhodes (0.32), MSRA Fellowship (0.34) all sit below the long-tail-OpenAlex working-researcher baseline (0.46). Olympic-style credentials no longer propagate names through the LLM channel. See the ladder →
  2. Artifact > creator inversion. In 8 of 11 verified (creator, artifact) pairs, the artifact outranks its creator. For independent solo creators (Datasette, nanoGPT, Tianshou, LangChain, Cursor) the inversion is uniform. Explore the pairs →
  3. h-index ≫ raw citations. log(h-index) explains R²=0.40 of NameRank variance for OpenAlex researchers; log(citations) explains 0.14 with zero marginal value beyond h-index. Methodology →
  4. Corpus-density gradient. Stanford CS faculty mean NameRank 0.54 vs. Tsinghua 0.26. Country effects place Israel/Singapore/Sweden above the USA baseline. See the country chart →
  5. C6 falsification. tuixue.online (∼1M Chinese-language users) registers NameRank 0.25, less than half of nanoGPT (0.71), with a flat cross-language null. Cross-language deltas →
