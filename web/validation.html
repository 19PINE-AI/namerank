Validation & diagnostics

The numbers behind the metric: per-model generosity and refusal rates, how scores stabilize as the panel size grows, and how much the score lifts when you condition on any model recognized this entity. These are the appendix tables of the paper, made interactive.

Panel-size sensitivity

For each panel size k, we sample 100 random k-model subsets and report the Pearson and Spearman correlation against the full 37-model NameRank. By k = 10, the correlation against the full panel is already above 0.94; by k = 20, above 0.98. This is the empirical justification for a fixed 37-model panel as a stable estimator of corpus presence.

Per-model generosity and refusal

One row per panel model. Generosity is the unweighted mean of cov × acc across all 5,719 entities; refusal is the fraction of entities the model answered "unknown". Thinking-mode variants are flagged. The strict cluster is dominated by smaller models and by RL-tuned-against-hallucination defaults; the generous cluster is the frontier-reasoning subset. Variance across this column is what the 37-model panel mean is averaging over.

Conditional NameRank: cohort recognition rate

Per cohort, unconditional NameRank is the headline mean across all entities and models; conditional NameRank is the mean restricted to (entity, model) pairs where the model did not refuse. The recognition rate is the fraction of model responses that produced some non-refusal content. The ratio conditional / unconditional measures how much of a cohort's low NameRank is driven by refusal rather than poor-quality answers — the GPT-5 system-card author cohort lifts 2.3× when we restrict to non-refusing responses, but its conditional score is still only 0.09.
